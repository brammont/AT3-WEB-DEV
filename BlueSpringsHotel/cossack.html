Cossack

Cossack, Western Australia (Bajinhurrba)

Cossack is a historic town located in Western Australia, known for its rich heritage and stunning coastal views. Explore the town's heritage buildings, museums, and scenic walking trails. Visit the Cossack Art Awards and immerse yourself in the local arts scene.

Immerse yourself in history at the Cossack Historic Town

Discover Cossack's history as a remnant port town with restored colonial buildings like the Commissariat Store and Old Courthouse. Visit the Cossack Museum to learn about the town's past.

Relax on the idyllic Settler's Beach

Beautiful beach with white sand and clear waters perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing. Enjoy stunning scenery, take a leisurely stroll, and witness the Staircase to the Moon during the dry season from April to October

Explore the underwater world

Abundant marine life in clear waters around Cossack make it ideal for snorkeling, diving, and spotting coral reefs, fish, turtles. Boat tours available.

Towns and Hamlets within 150 km of Cossack

Here are some towns and hamlets located within 150 km of Cossack:

  • Roebourne - Known for its rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Dampier - Home to the famous Red Dog statue and Dampier Archipelago.
  • Wickham - A town built to support the iron ore industry.
  • Point Samson - Known for its beautiful beaches and relaxed atmosphere.
  • Whim Creek - A small locality known for its historic pub.
